Audience Strategy

One Company.
Many Decision Makers.

Within a single target organisation, multiple people influence the decision to implement MetisJean. Each role has different fears, different language, and different content needs. We speak to all of them — differently.

CEO / MD

Strategic Owner

Fears strategic risk and losing competitive edge. Thinks in assets, equity, and moats. Needs to understand ownership, not technology.

CTO / Head of Engineering

Technical Evaluator

Fears bad architecture decisions and vendor risk. Thinks in systems, APIs, and deployment. Needs to understand how it works and why it is better.

COO / Head of Operations

Process Owner

Fears disruption and failed implementations. Thinks in workflows, efficiency, and ROI. Needs to understand the journey and what changes internally.

CFO / Finance Lead

Investment Gatekeeper

Fears wasted spend and unclear ROI. Thinks in costs, assets, and returns. Needs to understand why this is an investment, not a subscription.

Same Product. Same Feature. Different Message.

Example: The Sovereignty Score
CEO — Strategic Content
LinkedIn Post
"Your competitors are building AI assets. You're paying rent."
Every month you use ChatGPT Enterprise, Microsoft Copilot, or any external AI — you're paying for intelligence that disappears the moment you stop. No equity. No moat. No ownership. MetisJean's Sovereignty Score tracks your journey from 5% to 95% AI ownership. Because the companies that own their intelligence will outcompete the ones that rent it. Which are you building?
Pain: competitive risk · zero equity built · strategic dependency
What the CEO fears: Competitors gaining an intelligence advantage while they are renting from the same providers. The Sovereignty Score speaks to capital allocation — turning AI from a cost line into a balance sheet asset.
CTO — Technical Content
LinkedIn Post
"What happens to your AI pipeline when OpenAI changes its API?"
In February 2026, Claude Code broke for complex engineering tasks after a model update. 519 GitHub issues in 48 hours. Teams blocked. Deadlines missed. This is what vendor dependency looks like at scale. MetisJean deploys on your infrastructure — your own LLMs, your own knowledge graph, your own agents. When external providers change, break, or shut down, your operations continue. Architecture that you own cannot be deprecated by someone else's roadmap decision.
Pain: vendor risk · API dependency · architectural fragility
What the CTO fears: Building critical infrastructure on APIs they do not control. The on-premise deployment story speaks directly to architectural sovereignty — no external dependencies means no external failure modes.
